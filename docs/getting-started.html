Chapter I
Getting Started

Game Overview

Welcome, young Paleontologist! DinoCard is a dinosaur card collecting and battling game where you open packs, build a collection, and battle your prehistoric champions against CPU opponents. Your goal: collect all 11 dinosaurs and master the art of combat.

The game has three core modes, accessible from the main navigation:

Open Packs
Spend your packs to receive 5 random dinosaur cards. Every pack is guaranteed at least one Excavated (uncommon) or higher rarity card. Win battles to earn more packs!
Collection
View all dinosaurs you've collected. Cards show their name, type, rarity, stats, and how many copies you own. Build toward the complete set of all 11 dinosaurs!
Battle
Select one of your owned dinosaurs and face off against a CPU opponent. The CPU picks a dinosaur within one rarity tier of yours. Win to earn a free pack! See Chapter II for full battle details.

Opening Packs

Each pack contains exactly 5 cards. Cards are drawn randomly based on rarity weights:

Rarity Drop Rate Cards in Tier
Fossilized 55% 3 (Velociraptor, Iguanodon, Stegosaurus)
Excavated 25% 3 (Ankylosaurus, Dilophosaurus, Parasaurolophus)
Ancient 12% 2 (Triceratops, Carnotaurus)
Apex 6% 2 (Spinosaurus, T-Rex)
Extinction 2% 1 (Giganotosaurus)
Trainer Tip: Pity System
The 5th card in every pack is guaranteed to be at least Excavated rarity if none of the first 4 cards were uncommon or better. You'll never open a pack of all commons!

Building Your Collection

There are 11 dinosaurs to collect across 5 rarity tiers. Your collection persists between sessions thanks to local storage.

Earning Packs

You start with a set of packs, and earn 1 additional pack every time you win a battle. This is the core gameplay loop:

Open Packs → Get Cards → Battle → Win Pack → Repeat!
Trainer Tip: Barcode Scanner
The battle mode includes a barcode scanner feature! Scan any barcode to summon a specific enemy dinosaur using seeded randomization. The same barcode always produces the same opponent — great for practicing specific matchups.

Dinosaur Types

Every dinosaur belongs to one of two types. While there are no type advantages in battle damage calculations, types affect playstyle:

Carnivore

High Attack, Lower Defense

Glass cannons. Carnivores hit hard but can't take as many hits. Best used aggressively with Strike and Power Strike.

Herbivore

High Defense, Higher HP

Tanks. Herbivores absorb damage and outlast opponents. Best used with Guard and well-timed Power Strikes.

Important Note
There are no type advantages or weaknesses in DinoCard's damage formula. Carnivore vs. Herbivore doesn't change damage. What matters is the raw ATK, DEF, and HP stats — and your move choices!
