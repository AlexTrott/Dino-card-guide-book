Chapter V
David vs. Goliath

Think you can't win with a common card? Think again. This chapter reveals the exact strategies to topple titans with the tiniest fighters. Every matchup here is possible thanks to DinoCard's energy system and the power of well-timed Power Strikes.

How to Read This Chapter
Each matchup below includes full damage calculations, a difficulty rating, an honest win-rate estimate, and a “dream scenario” turn-by-turn breakdown showing the best possible outcome. Remember: underdog wins require favorable CPU behavior — they are never guaranteed.

Matchup 1

Velociraptor vs Giganotosaurus — The Impossible Dream
Velociraptor
ATK 40 · DEF 25 · HP 60
VS
Giganotosaurus
ATK 110 · DEF 70 · HP 150

Difficulty: EXTREME

Estimated Win Rate: <5%

Damage Breakdown
Scenario Damage Note

Velociraptor Power Strike vs Giga 49 max(1, 60 − 10.5)
Giga Strike vs Velociraptor 80 ONE-SHOTS! (80 > 60 HP)
Giga Power Strike vs Velociraptor 161 MEGA one-shot
Giga Strike vs Velociraptor (guarded) 40 Survivable, but barely
The Hard Truth
Giganotosaurus one-shots Velociraptor with a single unguarded Strike (80 damage vs 60 HP). You must Guard every single turn Giga might Strike. Your only meaningful damage comes from Power Strikes dealing 49 each. You need 4 Power Strikes to win (4 × 49 = 196 > 150). This requires perfect prediction of when Giga will Hold versus Strike — and an extraordinary amount of luck.

Key Strategy

The entire strategy revolves around exploiting CPU AI energy patterns (covered in detail in Chapter VI):

  • When Giga has 2+ energy, it favors Strike or Power Strike — you MUST Guard.
  • When Giga just used Power Strike and has 0–1 energy, it must Hold to recharge — this is your window to Power Strike.
  • Guard when Giga has reserves. Power Strike when Giga is holding.

The problem: even one misprediction kills you. A single unguarded Strike ends the run instantly. This is the hardest underdog matchup in the entire game.

Dream Scenario (Everything Goes Right)

Turn Your Move Giga's Move Result
1 Guard (E: 2→2) Strike Take 40 dmg. HP: 20
2 Power Strike (E: 2→1) Hold (recharging) Deal 49. Giga HP: 101
3 Guard (E: 1→1) Strike Take 40 dmg. HP: −20. DEAD.
Even the Dream Fails

The math is merciless. Even guarding Giga's Strikes, you take 40 damage each time. With 60 HP, you can only survive one guarded Strike before you're in lethal range. To land 4 Power Strikes, Giga would need to use Hold or Guard on at least 4 separate turns — and you'd need to Guard every single one of Giga's attacks without error.

Here is the absolute miracle scenario, requiring Giga to Hold four turns in a row:

Turn Your Move Giga's Move Result
1 Power Strike (E: 2→1) Hold Deal 49. Giga HP: 101
2 Hold (E: 1→2) Hold No damage. Saving energy.
3 Power Strike (E: 2→1) Guard Deal 49. Giga HP: 52
4 Hold (E: 1→2) Hold No damage. Saving energy.
5 Power Strike (E: 2→1) Guard Deal 49. Giga HP: 3
6 Strike (E: 1→1) Hold Deal 11. Giga HP: 0. WIN!
Reality Check
This miracle scenario requires Giga to never Strike or Power Strike for 6 straight turns. The CPU AI will almost certainly attack within the first 2 turns. If you attempt this matchup, treat every win as a badge of honor — and every loss as the expected outcome.

Dilophosaurus vs T-Rex — Venom vs Might

The Silver Lining
Dilophosaurus's Power Strike does a massive 73 damage. Two Power Strikes deal 146 — more than enough to topple T-Rex's 130 HP. You can two-shot the king of dinosaurs. The catch? T-Rex nearly one-shots you with a plain Strike (67 vs your 65 HP). One unguarded hit and it's over.

The Game Plan

You need exactly two Power Strikes to win. The challenge is surviving long enough to land both while managing your energy. Here's the painful reality of each possible opening:

The Dilemma

Path A — Guard first: Guard Turn 1 (take 33 guarded, HP 32). Now you're at 32 HP with 2 energy. You can Power Strike Turn 2 for 73 damage — but if T-Rex Strikes, you take 67 and die at −35 HP.

Path B — Power Strike first: Power Strike Turn 1, deal 73 (T-Rex HP: 57). But if T-Rex Strikes, you take 67 and die immediately.

Either path requires T-Rex to not Strike on a critical turn. This is why the win rate is only ~15%.

Win Condition

T-Rex must use a non-Strike action (Hold, Guard, or be mid-Power-Strike setup) on Turns 2 and 3 of the optimal sequence. You Guard the first attack, then go all-in with Power Strikes while praying T-Rex pauses.

Dream Scenario

Turn Your Move T-Rex's Move Result
1 Guard (E: 2→2) Strike Take 33 dmg. HP: 32
2 Power Strike (E: 2→1) Hold Deal 73. T-Rex HP: 57
3 Hold (E: 1→2) Guard No damage. Building energy.
4 Power Strike (E: 2→1) Hold Deal 73. T-Rex HP: 0. WIN!
Critical Vulnerability
After Guarding Turn 1, you sit at 32 HP. A single T-Rex Strike on Turn 2, 3, or 4 kills you (67 > 32). Even a guarded Strike (33) would leave you at −1 HP on Turn 3 or 4. Once you take that first guarded hit, you are living on borrowed time with zero margin for error. T-Rex must choose Hold or Guard on Turns 2, 3, and 4 for you to survive.
Trainer Tip: The YOLO Open
Some players skip the Guard and open with Power Strike on Turn 1. If T-Rex also Holds or Guards, you deal 73 immediately and only need one more Power Strike. It's riskier on Turn 1 (instant death if T-Rex Strikes) but reduces the total turns you need to survive. High risk, high reward — fitting for a David vs. Goliath fight.

Matchup 3

Stegosaurus vs Spinosaurus — Plates vs Sail

Why This Matchup Has Hope
Unlike the previous matchups, Stegosaurus's 50 DEF actually matters. Spino's Strike deals 53 — painful, but not lethal from full HP. Guarding reduces it to just 26. With 70 HP, you can survive two guarded Strikes (52 total). Your Power Strike does 36 damage, meaning you need 4 Power Strikes to win (4 × 36 = 144 > 120). It's a war of attrition.

The Problem

You can survive two guarded Strikes (26 + 26 = 52, leaving you at 18 HP), but the moment Spino lands one unguarded Strike (53), you're likely dead. And you need to find windows for four Power Strikes across approximately 8 turns. That's a long time to stay alive.

Spino's Power Strike is Instant Death
Spino's Power Strike deals 120 damage — more than your full 70 HP. Guard does NOT reduce Power Strike damage. If Spino uses Power Strike on any turn, you die unless you happened to Strike or Power Strike and finish Spino off first. Watch Spino's energy: if it has 2+ energy and hasn't attacked recently, a Power Strike may be coming.

Dream Scenario

This requires Spino to Guard or Hold at critical moments, giving you openings for Power Strikes while you soak hits with Guard on the dangerous turns.

1 Guard (E: 2→2) Strike Take 26 dmg. HP: 44
2 Power Strike (E: 2→1) Hold Deal 36. Spino HP: 84
3 Guard (E: 1→1) Strike Take 26 dmg. HP: 18
4 Power Strike (E: 1→0) Guard Deal 36. Spino HP: 48

Reality Check
This dream scenario requires Spino to Strike only on turns you Guard, and to Hold or Guard on turns you Power Strike. After Turn 3 you're at 18 HP — a single unguarded Spino Strike (53 damage) or any Power Strike (120 damage) kills you instantly. Spino must avoid attacking for six of nine turns. The CPU AI rarely cooperates this much. Expect to lose this fight the vast majority of the time.

General Underdog Tips

Five Rules for Fighting Above Your Weight
  1. Guard is your best friend. It halves Strike damage and costs only 1 energy. When in doubt, Guard. A halved hit is always better than a full one.
  2. Power Strike is your only meaningful damage. Against high-DEF opponents, regular Strikes deal single-digit damage. Power Strikes bypass most of their defense and are the only way to deal real damage in underdog fights.
  3. Watch the CPU's energy. If the opponent has 0–1 energy, it cannot use Power Strike. That makes Guard less valuable (since you only need to fear a regular Strike or nothing). Use these turns to Hold and bank energy.
  4. Hold when the opponent Guards. If you suspect the CPU will Guard, don't waste energy on a Strike that gets halved. Hold instead, save your energy, and set up a devastating Power Strike next turn.
  5. Accept that underdog wins require favorable CPU behavior. These are not guaranteed strategies. They are optimized plans that give you the best possible chance, but that chance is still slim. Celebrate every underdog victory — you earned it.
The Underdog Mindset
The best underdog players don't get frustrated by losses. They study the CPU's patterns (Chapter VI), learn to predict Hold windows, and strike with surgical precision. Even a 5% win rate means you'll get that legendary victory every 20 attempts. The story of that one time your Velociraptor felled a Giganotosaurus? That's what DinoCard memories are made of.
