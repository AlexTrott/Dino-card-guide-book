Chapter II
Battle System

The Four Moves

Every battle turn, both you and the CPU simultaneously choose one of four moves. Each move costs a certain amount of energy from your reserves.

Move Cost Effect
⚔ Strike 1 Energy Standard attack. Deals damage based on your ATK vs opponent's DEF. Damage is halved if the opponent uses Guard.
💥 Power Strike 2 Energy Devastating attack with 1.5x ATK multiplier. Ignores Guard entirely! Only slightly reduced by DEF. The most powerful move in the game.
🛡 Guard 1 Energy Defensive stance. Deals no damage, but halves incoming Strike damage. Does NOT block Power Strike! Costs 1 energy to use.
⏲ Hold 0 Energy Do nothing. Deals no damage and provides no defense. But costs nothing — use this to save energy for a future Power Strike.
Trainer Tip: The Core Mind Game
DinoCard's battle system is like rock-paper-scissors with resource management:
  • Strike beats Hold (deals damage while opponent wastes a turn)
  • Guard beats Strike (halves incoming damage)
  • Power Strike beats Guard (ignores it completely!)
  • Hold beats Guard (opponent wastes energy while you save)

Energy & Reserves

Your energy reserve is the lifeblood of battle. Managing it well is the difference between victory and extinction.

Energy Rules
  • Starting Reserve: Both fighters begin with 2 energy
  • Maximum Reserve: Energy caps at 4
  • Regeneration: You gain +1 energy at the end of every turn
  • Spending: You can only use moves you can afford

Here's how your energy flows through a typical opening:

Turn Move Energy Before After
1 Strike (-1) 2 2
2 Strike (-1) 2 2
3 Hold (0) 2 3
4 Power Strike (-2) 3 2
Trainer Tip: The Hold-Power Combo
The most efficient damage pattern is: Hold → Power Strike. Holding saves energy for a devastating Power Strike next turn. This is especially deadly with high-ATK carnivores.

Damage Formulas

Understanding exactly how damage is calculated gives you a massive edge. Here are the exact formulas from the game's source code:

Strike Damage
DMG = max(1, ATK × 0.8 − DEF × 0.3)

If the opponent uses Guard, the result is halved (rounded down, minimum 1).

Power Strike Damage
DMG = max(1, ATK × 1.5 − DEF × 0.15)

Guard has NO effect! Power Strike damage is never reduced by guarding. DEF still matters, but at only 0.15x instead of 0.3x.

Guard & Hold
DMG = 0

Neither Guard nor Hold deal any damage. Guard's value is purely defensive (halving Strike damage).

Critical Insight: Minimum Damage
All attacks deal at least 1 damage, even if the formula would produce 0 or negative. This means even the weakest dinosaur can eventually wear down the strongest — it just takes a LOT of turns. This is crucial for underdog strategies (see Chapter V).

Victory Conditions

A battle ends when either fighter's HP reaches 0. The survivor wins!

Victory Rewards

Winning a battle awards you +1 Pack. This is the primary way to earn new packs after your initial supply runs out.

CPU Matchmaking

The CPU picks an opponent within ±1 rarity tier of your selected dinosaur. This means:

Your Card's Rarity Possible CPU Rarity
Fossilized Fossilized or Excavated
Excavated Fossilized, Excavated, or Ancient
Ancient Excavated, Ancient, or Apex
Apex Ancient, Apex, or Extinction
Extinction Apex or Extinction
Trainer Tip: Easy Wins
Since matchmaking is random within the rarity range, you can sometimes face a lower-rarity opponent. Picking an Ancient card gives you a chance to face an Excavated opponent — a much easier fight for grinding packs!
